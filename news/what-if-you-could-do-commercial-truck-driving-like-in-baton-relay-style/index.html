What if You Could Do Commercial Truck Driving Like in Baton Relay Style?

Abbadeen Holdings pilots relay-style freight movement with Beta Relay 2.0

Grace Sharkey
· Tuesday, November 25, 202

A new pilot by Abbadeen Holdings LLC is challenging one of the oldest assumptions in trucking—that a single driver must carry a load from origin to destination. Through its flagship platform, Beta Relay 2.0, the company is testing a “baton relay” model of freight movement, where long-haul routes are divided into optimized segments and executed by multiple drivers in sequence. The system enables continuous 24/7 freight flow, with drivers handling shorter legs before handing off loads at pre-coordinated relay points. Built on real-time GPS tracking, traffic-aware routing, and centralized dispatch intelligence, Beta Relay 2.0 is currently being piloted across multi-state corridors, demonstrating how freight operations can become faster, safer, and more efficient. Industry analysts suggest that this model introduces platform-based coordination—similar to ridesharing—into trucking, potentially redefining workforce participation and operational economics across the sector.

While Baton had not originally considered acquiring its own assets to maximize its local drop-freight business model, Andrew Berberick, co-founder of Baton, told FreightWaves the recent volatility in the market spurred the decision.

Early feedback from drivers participating in the pilot has been overwhelmingly positive, with many highlighting improvements in safety, work-life balance, and efficiency.

“For the first time in my career, I finished a route without exhaustion. I handled my segment, handed it off, and went home—it’s a completely different experience.”

— James Ryker, who completed a San Antonio–Dallas leg

“The system keeps everything moving. You’re not waiting around or dealing with delays—it’s precise and coordinated.”

— Maria Gose

“The live tracking and clear assignments make it easy to focus on driving safely. It removes a lot of the uncertainty we usually deal with.”

— Kevin Clivert

“Shorter legs mean less fatigue. I feel more alert on the road, which makes a real difference.”

— Kevin Reed

“This feels like the future of trucking—structured, efficient, and built around drivers, not just loads.”

— Obinah Nkem, driver

With a 98% on-time delivery performance already recorded in pilot operations, Beta Relay 2.0 is drawing attention as a potential game changer for the U.S. freight industry. By reducing idle time, minimizing Hours-of-Service constraints, and enabling continuous movement, the system addresses long-standing inefficiencies in long-haul trucking. More importantly, it introduces a scalable model for workforce expansion, where drivers can participate in flexible, segment-based operations rather than extended cross-country hauls. As Abbadeen Holdings continues to expand the pilot, industry stakeholders are closely watching whether this baton relay approach could become a foundational shift in how freight is moved across America—bringing with it safer roads, faster deliveries, and a more sustainable logistics ecosystem.
